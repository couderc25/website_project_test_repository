Beans

        Ingreds:
        Around 15 oz dry black beans
        2 bay leaves
        ~2 tbsp salt
        ~2-3 tbsp oil— virgin or very virgin only. no whores
        ½ an onion
        ~8 cloves garlic
        Pepper 
        
        Recipe:
        
        - Rinse beans 
        - Soak beans overnight in fridge in as much water as there are beans (cook time ~2 hrs)
        - Alternatively, be lazy and don’t soak them (cook time ~3-3.5 hrs)
        - If you soaked them, add water so that beans are covered about 1/4 inch 
        - If you didn’t soak them, add as much water as there are beans. 
        - Add 2 bay leaves if u have em and cook over medium low heat with lid cracked (about a 3 on the stove)
        - Either way, check water often as beans cook and make sure they’re always at least ¼ inch covered. Water cooks off very fast in the beginning
        
        - Separately:
        - In a frying pan, add the diced ½ onion, 8 cloves of garlic, ~1 tbsp salt , lots of pepper, and ~2-3tbps virgin
        - I’m not sure exactly how much oil but the onion and garlic should be kind of swimming in it rather than just wetted by it
        
        Add the onion/garlic/ oil mixture to beans when beans are soft and basically done cooking. Cook for another 15-30 and add salt if needed

Butternut Squash Chicken Curry

        Butternut squash - 1 big one or 2-3 bags of frozen chunks (or more if you want more)
        Chicken - 1 package skinless boneless thighs or breasts, chopped into bite size chunks 
        1 bunch spinach, chopped, or a bag or tub of baby spinach 
        1 onion, diced
        2-3 cloves garlic, minced
        1can coconut milk (or you can use dairy cream)
        1can diced tomatoes
        Fresh grated ginger 1 tbsp?
        Ghee, butter or olive oil
        Mustard seeds 1-2 Tbsp
        Salt
        Curry powder
        Garam masala (or just ground cumin)
        
        Chop up squash and onion. 
        Heat oil in pan and put in mustard seeds - let cook until they start jumping and popping. 
        Add onion and garlic and stir till translucent
        Add curry powder and stir to coat. Add squash, chicken, tomatoes and their juice and ginger and salt. Cook on med low, stirring every now and then until soft. 
        Add coconut milk and heat through. 
        Taste and add salt if needed. 
        Add spinach and cook just until wilted 5 minutes or less. Sprinkle some garam masala or ground cumin in, stir and serve.
